Industries

TEAM Automation Berlin solutions are applied in many industries and can be flexibly adapted to the specific requirements of different sectors. Whether it is the automotive industry, electrical engineering, medical technology, or packaging technology – TEAM Automation Berlin offers tailored automation solutions that increase efficiency, precision, and quality in your production. Industry Overview

Automotive

The automotive industry is one of TEAM Automation Berlin’s key sectors. Nearly 20 years of experience in this field has made TEAM a reliable and strong partner for many renowned suppliers. TEAM Automation Berlin offers complete, ready-to-use assembly lines – from the assembly of individual components to final product inspection – that are technologically advanced and economically viable: intelligent automation for the mobility of the future.

Electrical Engineering

TEAM Automation has designed fully automated assembly lines for contact equipment of various telecommunications connectors.

Medical

Medical technology plays an increasingly significant role at TEAM Automation Berlin. Examples include packaging lines for syringes, fully automated assembly systems for asthma inhalers, and testing stations for tattoo needles.

Solar Energy

For one of its largest customers, TEAM Automation Berlin built fully automated assembly lines for mounting photovoltaic modules. TEAM also participates in projects related to recycling used modules and material recovery.

Chemicals

In the chemical industry, TEAM Automation Berlin has gained experience through a fully automated line for dispensing and filling dosing caps and basins. After filling, the caps were rinsed with argon and sealed with an aluminum sealing film.

Battery Management

Battery management is also part of TEAM Automation Berlin’s portfolio. For example, semi-automated lines for assembly, testing, and labeling of battery packs have been implemented.

Safety Technology

TEAM Automation Berlin has developed a fully automated testing system for a client using robots for smoke detectors and a dual processing station with robots for sensor components. This includes automatic loading and unloading of test sockets, labeling of verified components, and process data recording.
